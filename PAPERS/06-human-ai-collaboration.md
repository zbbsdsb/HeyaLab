## 1. Human-AI Collaboration Patterns

### 1.1 Advancing Human-Machine Teaming: Concepts, Challenges, and Applications
- **Authors**: Dian Chen, Han Jun Yoon, Zelin Wan et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Core Contribution**: Proposes a comprehensive classification system for human-machine teaming (HMT), covering theoretical models such as reinforcement learning, instance learning, and interdependence theory
- **Key Technologies**: HMT taxonomy, trust calibration, adaptive teaming, team cognition theory
- **Heya Relevance**: **Theoretical Framework** - Classification basis for Heya human-AI collaboration patterns
- **Link**: [arXiv:2503.16518](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16518)

### 1.2 A Design Trajectory Map of Human-AI Collaborative Reinforcement Learning Systems
- **Authors**: Zhaoxing Li
- **Year**: 2024
- **Core Contribution**: First systematic survey of collaborative reinforcement learning (CRL), building a taxonomy based on interactive RL algorithms and human-AI collaboration frameworks
- **Key Technologies**: Collaborative reinforcement learning (CRL), human-AI interaction framework, taxonomy
- **Heya Relevance**: **Adaptive Adoption** - Heya human-AI feedback learning mechanism
- **Link**: [arXiv:2405.10214](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.10214)

### 1.3 Unpacking Interaction Profiles and Strategies in Human-AI Collaborative Problem Solving
- **Authors**: Zhanxin Hao, Xiaobo Liu, Jiaxin Fan et al.
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Identifies three human-AI collaboration modes: Delegated Reasoning (DR), Collaborative Interpretation (CI), and Delegated Elaboration (DE)
- **Key Technologies**: Distributed cognition theory, learning regulation theory, collaboration mode classification
- **Heya Relevance**: **Direct Adoption** - Pattern reference for Heya Checkpoint types
- **Link**: [arXiv:2603.21288](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21288)

### 1.4 Human Machine Social Hybrid Intelligence: A Collaborative Decision Making Framework
- **Authors**: Ahmet Akkaya Melih, Yamuna Singh et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Core Contribution**: Proposes the Human-Machine Social Hybrid Intelligence (HMS-HI) framework, addressing cognitive overload caused by Human-in-the-Loop in complex high-risk environments
- **Key Technologies**: Human-in-the-Loop, multi-agent systems, cognitive overload mitigation
- **Heya Relevance**: **Directly Related** - Heya human-AI collaboration design for high-risk decisions
- **Link**: [arXiv:2510.24030](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24030)

### 1.5 Interface Framework for Human-AI Collaboration within Intelligent User Interface Ecosystems
- **Authors**: Shruthi Andru, Shrut Kirti Saksena
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Proposes a dimensional framework based on workflow complexity, AI autonomy, and AI reasoning capability to guide context-aware AI interface design
- **Key Technologies**: Dimensional framework, AI autonomy grading, context-aware interfaces
- **Heya Relevance**: **Adaptive Adoption** - Design dimensions for Heya Checkpoint UI
- **Link**: [arXiv:2602.22343](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22343)

## 2. Cognitive Load and Transparency

### 2.1 The Transparency Paradox in Explainable AI: A Theory of Autonomy Depletion Through Cognitive Load
- **Authors**: Ancuta Margondai, Mustapha Mouloua
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Discovers that transparency is not universally beneficial -- the same AI explanations improve decisions in some contexts but impair them in others. Models autonomy as a continuous random variable
- **Key Technologies**: Cognitive load theory, autonomy depletion model, transparency paradox, trust calibration theory
- **Heya Relevance**: **Direct Adoption** - Theoretical foundation for P012 Cognitive Load Manager
- **Link**: [arXiv:2601.13973](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13973)

### 2.2 Revising Bloom's Taxonomy for Dual-Mode Cognition in Human-AI Systems
- **Authors**: Kayode P. Ayodele, Enoruwa Obayiuwana et al.
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Revises Bloom's taxonomy to accommodate dual-mode cognition in human-AI systems (independent biological cognition vs. distributed human-AI cognition)
- **Key Technologies**: Bloom's taxonomy revision, dual-mode cognition, augmented cognition framework
- **Heya Relevance**: **Reference** - Assessment framework for human-AI distributed cognition
- **Link**: [arXiv:2602.00697](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00697)

### 2.3 Generative AI User Experience: Developing Human-AI Epistemic Partnership
- **Authors**: Xiaoming Zhai
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Proposes an "epistemic partnership" framework to explain experiences such as negotiated authority, cognitive redistribution, and cognitive load
- **Key Technologies**: Epistemic partnership, knowledge construction, cognitive redistribution
- **Heya Relevance**: **Reference** - Theoretical foundation for Heya human-AI cognitive division of labor
- **Link**: [arXiv:2603.23863](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23863)

## 3. Trust Calibration

### 3.1 Learning to Trust: How Humans Mentally Recalibrate AI Confidence Signals
- **Authors**: ZhaoBin Li, Mark Steyvers
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Behavioral experiments (N=200) studying how humans mentally recalibrate AI confidence signals through repeated experience
- **Key Technologies**: Trust calibration experiments, AI confidence signals, mental recalibration model
- **Heya Relevance**: **Direct Adoption** - Empirical foundation for P010 Trust Score Model
- **Link**: [arXiv:2603.22634](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22634)

### 3.2 The Persuasion Paradox: When LLM Explanations Fail to Improve Human-AI Team Performance
- **Authors**: Ruth Cohen, Lu Feng, Ayala Bloch, Sarit Kraus
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Discovers the "persuasion paradox" -- fluent LLM explanations systematically increase user confidence and reliance on AI without reliably improving task accuracy
- **Key Technologies**: Persuasion paradox, LLM explanations, trust over-calibration
- **Heya Relevance**: **Directly Related** - Design warning against over-trust in Heya
- **Link**: [arXiv:2604.03237](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03237)

### 3.3 From Accuracy to Readiness: Metrics and Benchmarks for Human-AI Decision-Making
- **Authors**: Min Hun Lee
- **Year**: 2026
- **Core Contribution**: Proposes a team-readiness-centered human-AI decision evaluation framework, noting that current evaluation focuses too heavily on model accuracy
- **Key Technologies**: Team readiness evaluation framework, trust calibration metrics
- **Heya Relevance**: **Adaptive Adoption** - Heya human-AI team effectiveness evaluation
- **Link**: [arXiv:2603.18895](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18895)

### 3.4 Gaze-informed Signatures of Trust and Collaboration in Human-Autonomy Teams
- **Authors**: Anthony J. Ries, Stephane Aroca-Ouellette et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **Core Contribution**: Studies trust and collaboration patterns under different AI agent behaviors (clumsy, rigid, adaptive) and environmental complexity
- **Key Technologies**: Hierarchical reinforcement learning, human-autonomy teaming (HAT), trust dynamics, adaptive agents
- **Heya Relevance**: **Reference** - Impact of adaptive AI behavior on trust
- **Link**: [arXiv:2409.19139](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.19139)

## Key Insights

### Human-AI Collaboration Design Principles (Based on Paper Research)

1. **Dynamic trust**: Trust is not static; it needs dynamic calibration based on AI performance and context
2. **Cognitive load management**: Information transparency has a paradox; adaptive adjustment is needed based on task complexity
3. **Autonomy spectrum**: Human-AI collaboration is not a binary choice but a continuous spectrum from fully manual to fully autonomous
4. **Bias awareness**: Systematic cognitive biases exist in human-AI interaction, requiring proactive identification and mitigation
5. **Team readiness**: Evaluating human-AI collaboration should not focus solely on model accuracy but on overall team readiness
6. **Simple design**: Follow the "Don't Make Me Think" principle to make interfaces intuitive and easy to use
7. **Progressive disclosure**: Present information in layers, avoid showing too much at once
